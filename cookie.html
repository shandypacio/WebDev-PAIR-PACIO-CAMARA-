Classic Chocolate Chip Cookie

A classic chocolate chip cookie is the epitome of comfort food—soft and chewy on the inside with a slightly crispy edge. It’s made with a rich, buttery dough that’s perfectly balanced between sweet and salty, studded generously with semi-sweet chocolate chips that melt into gooey pockets of decadence when baked. The golden-brown exterior has just the right amount of crunch, while the interior remains soft and moist. This timeless treat is a perfect harmony of flavors and textures, making it a universally beloved dessert or snack.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons hot water
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Instructions

Step 1.

Gather your ingredients, making sure your butter is softened, and your eggs are room temperature.

Step 2.

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Beat butter, white sugar, and brown sugar with an electric mixer in a large bowl until smooth.

Step 3.

Beat in eggs, one at a time, then stir in vanilla.

Step 4.

Dissolve baking soda in hot water. Add to batter along with salt.

Step 5.

Stir in flour, chocolate chips, and walnuts.

Step 6.

Drop spoonfuls of dough 2 inches apart onto ungreased baking sheets.

Step 7.

Bake in the preheated oven until the edges are nicely browned, about 10 minutes.

Step 8.

Cool on the baking sheets briefly before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.

Step 9.

Store in an airtight container or serve immediately and enjoy!
